Welcome

I am a graduate fellow in the Department of Language and Linguistics at the University of Tromsø, Norway. I am a member of the CLEAR research group, and I closely collaborate with the Giellatekno group for my computer-related research. I also work closely with a few members of the Department of Linguistics at the University Tübingen.

  

My work currently focuses on Russian Intelligent Computer-Assisted Language Learning (ICALL). I am most interested in the following topics:

  

  • I. Automatic Visual Input Enhancement

  • II. Automatic L2 readability analysis

  • III. Indexing online texts for language learners (e.g., language-aware search engine)

  • IV. Structure of Russian, especially as it relates to developing Russian ICALL activities

I began teaching Russian professionally in 2002. I received my MA degree in Russian Linguistics from The Ohio State University in 2011, and I am currently ABD, working on my PhD dissertation in Russian Computational Linguistics from the University of Tromsø.

  

For more detail, check out the contents of my CV in cv-reynolds.pdf (updated 22 Oct 2014).
